Three Rules for Our Collaboration¶

I want you to achieve a good outcome by the end of this course.
You can help me – and yourself – by following three simple principles:

  1. Openness instead of concealment
  2. Ask questions instead of staying silent
  3. Preparation instead of improvisation

Rule 1: Openness Instead of Concealment¶

It is hard for me to help when I do not know the actual background of a question or request.

When I sense that someone is being honest with me, I find a solution much more easily than when I carry the constant doubt of whether I am being misled – even if the cause of the problem was a mistake.
A solution will never be better if I feel deceived in finding it.

Rule 2: Ask Questions Instead of Staying Silent¶

Be active and speak up when I ask if there are any questions.
I cannot know what you do not tell me.

Silence signals understanding – and I will take that at face value.
If something is unclear: ask.

Rule 3: Preparation Instead of Improvisation¶

When you have a question, come prepared.
At a minimum: ask a fellow student first, or check OLAT again.

Those who come unprepared may even have difficulty understanding my answer.
Questions generated solely by an AI are not preparation – show me that you have thought about your question and that it genuinely asks what you need to know.

But: I am expressly open to questions!
